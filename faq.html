First Aid

First Aid and EMS services are available at the permanent restroom next to the tennis courts.

Baby Station

Need a quiet place to change your baby's diaper or to nurse? Visit the Festival's Baby Station, located near the Arty's Stage. Rocking chairs, fans, and other amenities are available to make sure our tiniest Festival-goers stay comfortable throughout the day.

  • Friday: 10 a.m. - 6 p.m.
  • Saturday: 10 a.m. - 6 p.m.
  • Sunday: 12 p.m. - 5 p.m.

Lost & Found

Visit the Festival's Lost & Found booth near the Fourth Street Bridge to scope out misplaced items. Lost children also are escorted here to be reunited with parents. Lost & Found is located south of Festival Headquarters.

Information Booth

The best source for directions and other Festival-related questions. T-shirts are sold here. Two locations: near the Kenwood Bridge and west of Craft Demonstration.

Shuttles

Free shuttles from the Tony's Pizza Events Center parking lot to Festival grounds are accessible.

  • Friday: 10 a.m. - 8:30 p.m.
  • Saturday: 10 a.m. - 8:30 p.m.
  • Sunday: 10 a.m. - 4 p.m.

Parking

Parking lots are adjacent to the Tony's Pizza Events Center and in the general vicinity of the Festival. No unauthorized vehicles or pets are allowed in the Park. Free Shuttle service is accessible and available from the Tony's Pizza Events Center parking lot to Festival grounds.

CityGo/OCCK Services

Free rides to the Festival on the CityGo Bus System are available to anyone wearing a Festival button (Thursday, Friday, Saturday). No Sunday service.

The CityGo Routes will offer expanded bus service on Thursday, Friday and Saturday from 6:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. to Oakdale Park (Oakdale entrance and Events Center foot bridge) during the Festival, to take people anywhere on normal routes within Salina City limits.

After regular shuttle hours, OCCK provides transportation for people with movement impairments, on-call basis only. Check in at Festival Headquarters for assistance. Friday and Saturday: 8:30 p.m. to 10:00 p.m.

Restrooms

Public rest rooms are available throughout the Park. Accessible rest rooms and hand washing stations also are available.

Ident-a-Kid

Calling all kids to stop by the Ident-A-Kid booth just west of Craft Demonstration to get your I.D. bracelet. It's the best way for us to find your mom or dad if you get separated. Have your folks bring their cell phone for easy access.

SMILE!

The Smoky Hill River Festival is documented each year by designated professional photographers hired by Festival staff. Their images are sometimes used appropriately for marketing purposes in subsequent years. Your purchase of an admission button gives permission for the use of your image in this fashion without notification or compensation. The Festival accepts no liability for any personal image used in this way.

Also, due to copyright infringement regulations for performing artists, absolutely NO unauthorized recording of any on grounds entertainment is permitted on Festival grounds for any reason.

Keep It Green

Look for receptacles to recycle plastic water bottles and metal or aluminum cans around the Festival, and to help reduce unnecessary landfill trash.
